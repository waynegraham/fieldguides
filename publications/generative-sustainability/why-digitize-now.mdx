Digitization is often viewed as a luxury or a secondary concern compared to physical preservation. However, in an era of increasing environmental volatility, digitization has become a primary strategy for risk mitigation and cultural continuity.

## Questions to Consider

Before embarking on a large-scale digitization project, institutions must ask critical questions about their goals, their capacity, and the environmental impact of their digital infrastructure:

- Which collections are at the highest risk from local climate threats?
- How can we ensure that digital surrogates are as accessible as possible?
- What is the carbon footprint of our storage and delivery systems?
- How do we prioritize community-held heritage that may not be in institutional collections?
